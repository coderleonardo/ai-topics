To train a BERT-like model,
you can use a technique called masked language modeling.
You first randomly mask a number of words from your input sequence
and have the model predict these masked words.
By doing so, the model learns to represent language
as it attempts to deconstruct these masked words.
Training is typically a two-step approach.
First, you apply masked language modeling on
large amounts of data, and this is called pre-training.
After which you can fine-tune your pre-trained model
on a number of downstream tasks, including classification.
